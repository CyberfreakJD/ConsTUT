Part V - Constitution of India

Study Material - Articles with Summaries

  • Article 52 - The President of India

    The President is the head of the Indian state and the executive authority of the Union.

  • Article 53 - Executive Power of the Union

    All executive powers of the Union are vested in the President but can be exercised by subordinate authorities.

  • Article 54 - Election of President

    The President is elected by an electoral college consisting of MPs and members of state legislative assemblies.

  • Article 55 - Manner of Election of President

    Provides details on the method of proportional representation and single transferable vote system for the election.

  • Article 56 - Term of Office of President

    The President's term is five years, and provisions for resignation and impeachment are also mentioned.

  • Article 57 - Eligibility for Re-election

    The President is eligible for re-election without any restrictions on the number of terms.

  • Article 58 - Qualifications for Election as President

    Specifies that the President must be a citizen of India, at least 35 years old, and qualified for election as a member of the Lok Sabha.

  • Article 59 - Conditions of President's Office

    Details about the conditions of service, including that the President shall not hold any other office of profit.

  • Article 60 - Oath or Affirmation by the President

    The President must take an oath or affirmation before assuming office, swearing to preserve, protect, and defend the Constitution.

  • Article 61 - Procedure for Impeachment of the President

    Provides the procedure for impeachment for violation of the Constitution.

  • Article 62 - Time of Holding Election to Fill Vacancy in the Office of President

    Discusses the election timing if the office of the President becomes vacant before the expiration of the term.

  • Article 63 - The Vice-President of India

    The Vice-President serves as the ex-officio Chairman of the Rajya Sabha and acts in the Presidentâ€™s absence.

  • Article 64 - The Vice-President to be Ex-Officio Chairman of the Council of States

    The Vice-President serves as the Chairman of the Rajya Sabha (Council of States).

  • Article 65 - The Vice-President to Act as President or to Discharge His Functions

    The Vice-President discharges the functions of the President during the latter's absence or incapacitation.

  • Article 66 - Election of Vice-President

    The Vice-President is elected by members of both Houses of Parliament.

  • Article 67 - Term of Office of Vice-President

    The Vice-President holds office for five years and is eligible for re-election.

  • Article 68 - Time of Holding Election to Fill Vacancy in the Office of Vice-President

    If the office becomes vacant, an election must be held to fill the position.

  • Article 69 - Oath or Affirmation by the Vice-President

    The Vice-President takes an oath before assuming office, affirming faith in the Constitution.

  • Article 70 - Discharge of President's Functions in Other Contingencies

    Provisions for handling situations in which both the President and Vice-President are unable to perform their duties.

  • Article 71 - Matters relating to, or Connected with, the Election of a President or Vice-President

    Details about disputes related to the election of the President or Vice-President, to be decided by the Supreme Court.

  • Article 72 - Power of President to Grant Pardons, etc., and to Suspend, Remit, or Commute Sentences

    The President has the power to grant pardons, reprieves, and commutations of sentences, including in death penalty cases.

  • Article 73 - Extent of Executive Power of the Union

    Defines the extent of the executive power vested in the Union government.

  • Article 74 - Council of Ministers to Aid and Advise the President

    The President acts in accordance with the advice provided by the Council of Ministers.

  • Article 75 - Other Provisions as to Ministers

    Provides for the appointment of ministers by the President on the advice of the Prime Minister, including their responsibilities and tenure.

  • Article 76 - Attorney-General for India

    The Attorney-General is the principal legal advisor to the Government of India.

  • Article 77 - Conduct of Business of the Government of India

    Stipulates that all executive action by the government is taken in the name of the President.

  • Article 78 - Duties of Prime Minister

    The Prime Minister has the duty to communicate to the President all decisions of the Council of Ministers relating to the administration of Union affairs.

  • Article 79 - Constitution of Parliament

    The Parliament consists of the President and two Housesâ€”the Council of States (Rajya Sabha) and the House of the People (Lok Sabha).

  • Article 80 - Composition of the Council of States

    The Council of States (Rajya Sabha) shall consist of not more than 250 members.

  • Article 81 - Composition of the House of the People

    The House of the People (Lok Sabha) consists of representatives of the states and union territories, not exceeding 552 members.

  • Article 82 - Readjustment after Each Census

    After every census, the allocation of seats to the states in the House of the People and the division of each state into territorial constituencies is readjusted.

  • Article 83 - Duration of Houses of Parliament

    The Council of States is a permanent body, while the House of the Peopleâ€™s term is five years unless dissolved earlier.

  • Article 84 - Qualifications for Membership of Parliament

    A person must be a citizen of India and at least 25 years of age for the House of the People and 30 for the Council of States.

  • Article 85 - Sessions of Parliament

    The President summons each House of Parliament at intervals of not more than six months.

  • Article 86 - Right of President to Address and Send Messages to Houses

    The President has the right to address either House of Parliament and to send messages to either House.

  • Article 87 - Special Address by the President

    The President addresses a joint session of both Houses at the beginning of the first session after each general election and at the first session of each year.

  • Article 88 - Rights of Ministers and Attorney-General as Respects Houses

    Ministers and the Attorney-General have the right to speak in both Houses of Parliament but are not entitled to vote if they are not members of the respective House.

  • Article 89 - The Chairman and Deputy Chairman of the Council of States

    The Vice-President of India serves as the ex-officio Chairman of the Rajya Sabha, and the Council elects a Deputy Chairman from among its members.

  • Article 90 - Vacation and Resignation of, and Removal from, the Office of Deputy Chairman

    Specifies the circumstances under which the Deputy Chairman vacates office, resigns, or is removed by a resolution of the Rajya Sabha.

  • Article 91 - Power of the Deputy Chairman or Other Person to Perform the Duties of the Office of, or to Act as, Chairman

    Specifies the procedure for the Deputy Chairman or another member to perform the duties of the Chairman when the office is vacant or the Chairman is absent.

  • Article 92 - The Chairman or the Deputy Chairman Not to Preside while a Resolution for His Removal from Office is under Consideration

    The Chairman or Deputy Chairman cannot preside over the House during discussions regarding a resolution for their removal.

  • Article 93 - The Speaker and Deputy Speaker of the House of the People

    The House of the People elects a Speaker and Deputy Speaker from among its members.

  • Article 94 - Vacation and Resignation of, and Removal from, the Offices of Speaker and Deputy Speaker

    Outlines the conditions for the Speaker and Deputy Speaker vacating or resigning from their offices, or their removal by resolution of the House.

  • Article 95 - Power of the Deputy Speaker or Other Person to Perform the Duties of the Office of, or to Act as, Speaker

    The Deputy Speaker or another member performs the duties of the Speaker in case of vacancy or absence of the Speaker.

  • Article 96 - The Speaker or the Deputy Speaker Not to Preside while a Resolution for His Removal from Office is under Consideration

    The Speaker or Deputy Speaker cannot preside over the House when a resolution for their removal is being discussed.

  • Article 97 - Salaries and Allowances of the Chairman and Deputy Chairman and the Speaker and Deputy Speaker

    Specifies the salaries and allowances for the Chairman and Deputy Chairman of the Rajya Sabha and the Speaker and Deputy Speaker of the Lok Sabha.

  • Article 98 - Secretariat of Parliament

    Each House of Parliament has its own secretarial staff, which is independent from the other House.

  • Article 99 - Oath or Affirmation by Members

    Members of Parliament must take an oath or affirmation before taking their seats in the House.

  • Article 100 - Voting in Houses, Power of Houses to Act Notwithstanding Vacancies, and Quorum

    Specifies voting procedures, how Houses can act even if there are vacancies, and the quorum required for business to be transacted in Parliament.

  • Article 101 - Vacation of Seats

    Outlines the conditions under which members of Parliament must vacate their seats, including holding office of profit or election to both Houses.

  • Article 102 - Disqualifications for Membership

    Lists the reasons for disqualification from being elected or serving as a member of Parliament, including holding office of profit, unsound mind, or insolvency.

  • Article 103 - Decision on Questions as to Disqualifications of Members

    The President decides on questions regarding the disqualification of members after consulting the Election Commission.

  • Article 104 - Penalty for Sitting and Voting before Making Oath or Affirmation under Article 99 or when Not Qualified or when Disqualified

    Provides penalties for members who sit and vote in Parliament without taking the oath or when they are disqualified or not qualified.

  • Article 105 - Powers, Privileges, and Immunities of Members of Parliament

    Defines the powers, privileges, and immunities of members of Parliament in matters of parliamentary proceedings.

  • Article 106 - Salaries and Allowances of Members

    Provides for the salaries and allowances of members of Parliament as determined by law.

  • Article 107 - Provisions as to Introduction and Passing of Bills

    Outlines the procedure for introducing and passing bills in Parliament.

  • Article 108 - Joint Sitting of Both Houses in Certain Cases

    Specifies the procedure for convening a joint sitting of both Houses to resolve disagreements on a bill.

  • Article 109 - Special Procedure in Respect of Money Bills

    Details the special procedure for the passage of money bills, which can only be introduced in the Lok Sabha and must receive the President's recommendation.

  • Article 110 - Definition of 'Money Bills'

    Defines money bills as those dealing with taxes, borrowing, expenditure, and related matters as specified in this article.

  • Article 111 - Assent to Bills

    The President must give assent to bills passed by both Houses, or withhold assent, or return the bill for reconsideration (except money bills).

  • Article 112 - Annual Financial Statement

    The President must present an annual financial statement (the budget) to Parliament, detailing estimated receipts and expenditure for the year.

  • Article 113 - Procedure in Parliament with Respect to Estimates

    Outlines the procedure for the discussion and voting of estimates of government expenditure in Parliament.

  • Article 114 - Appropriation Bills

    Defines the procedure for the passage of appropriation bills that provide legal sanction for government expenditure.

  • Article 115 - Supplementary, Additional, Excess Grants

    Provides for supplementary, additional, and excess grants when the amounts authorized for expenditure are insufficient or unanticipated expenditures arise.

  • Article 116 - Votes on Account, Votes of Credit, and Exceptional Grants

    Allows Parliament to approve provisional funding through a vote on account, a vote of credit for urgent needs, or exceptional grants.

  • Article 117 - Special Provisions as to Financial Bills

    Details the procedure for introducing and passing financial bills, distinct from money bills, but still dealing with financial matters.

  • Article 118 - Rules of Procedure

    Empowers each House of Parliament to make its own rules of procedure for conducting business, including committee formation and bill consideration.

  • Article 119 - Regulation by Law of Procedure in Parliament in Relation to Financial Business

    Parliament may regulate its financial business, including the discussion and voting on financial matters, by law.

  • Article 120 - Language to Be Used in Parliament

    Specifies that the business of Parliament shall be transacted in Hindi or English, with provisions for translation and interpretation.

  • Article 121 - Restriction on Discussion in Parliament

    No discussion shall take place in Parliament regarding the conduct of any judge of the Supreme Court or a High Court during discharge of duties, except in the case of impeachment.

  • Article 122 - Courts Not to Inquire into Proceedings of Parliament

    Courts cannot question the validity of any parliamentary proceedings on the grounds of procedural irregularity.

  • Article 123 - Power of President to Promulgate Ordinances during Recess of Parliament

    Authorizes the President to promulgate ordinances when Parliament is not in session, which have the force of law, but must be approved by Parliament within six weeks of reassembly.

  • Article 124 - Establishment and Constitution of Supreme Court

    Establishes the Supreme Court of India, its composition, and the process for appointing judges, including the Chief Justice of India.

  • Article 125 - Salaries, Etc., of Judges

    Provides for the determination of salaries, allowances, and rights in respect of leave of the judges of the Supreme Court.

  • Article 126 - Appointment of Acting Chief Justice

    Empowers the President to appoint an acting Chief Justice when the office of the Chief Justice of India is vacant or the Chief Justice is unable to perform duties.

  • Article 127 - Appointment of Ad Hoc Judges

    Provides for the appointment of ad hoc judges to the Supreme Court if a quorum of judges is not available.

  • Article 128 - Attendance of Retired Judges at Sittings of the Supreme Court

    Allows the Chief Justice of India to request retired Supreme Court or High Court judges to attend sittings of the Supreme Court.

  • Article 129 - Supreme Court to Be a Court of Record

    Declares that the Supreme Court is a court of record and has all the powers of such a court, including the power to punish for contempt of itself.

  • Article 130 - Seat of Supreme Court

    The Supreme Court shall sit in Delhi, but it may also sit in other places as designated by the Chief Justice of India with the approval of the President.

  • Article 131 - Original Jurisdiction of the Supreme Court

    The Supreme Court has original jurisdiction in disputes between the Government of India and one or more states or between states.

  • Article 132 - Appellate Jurisdiction of Supreme Court in Constitutional Cases

    The Supreme Court has appellate jurisdiction in cases involving substantial questions of law as to the interpretation of the Constitution.

  • Article 133 - Appellate Jurisdiction of Supreme Court in Appeals from High Courts in Civil Cases

    The Supreme Court has appellate jurisdiction in civil matters decided by High Courts where the case involves a substantial question of law.

  • Article 134 - Appellate Jurisdiction of Supreme Court in Appeals from High Courts in Criminal Cases

    Grants the Supreme Court the power to hear appeals in criminal matters decided by High Courts in certain specified cases.

  • Article 135 - Jurisdiction and Powers of the Federal Court under Existing Law to Be Exercised by the Supreme Court

    Provides for the transfer of the jurisdiction and powers of the Federal Court to the Supreme Court of India.

  • Article 136 - Special Leave to Appeal by the Supreme Court

    The Supreme Court has discretionary power to grant special leave to appeal against judgments from any court or tribunal in India.

  • Article 137 - Review of Judgments or Orders by the Supreme Court

    The Supreme Court has the power to review its own judgments or orders.

  • Article 138 - Enlargement of the Jurisdiction of the Supreme Court

    Parliament may, by law, confer additional jurisdiction on the Supreme Court beyond what is specified in the Constitution.

  • Article 139 - Conferment on the Supreme Court of Powers to Issue Certain Writs

    The Supreme Court is empowered to issue certain writs, such as habeas corpus, mandamus, prohibition, quo warranto, and certiorari, for the enforcement of fundamental rights.

  • Article 140 - Ancillary Powers of Supreme Court

    The Supreme Court is vested with such ancillary powers as may be necessary to effectively exercise its jurisdiction.

  • Article 141 - Law Declared by Supreme Court to Be Binding on All Courts

    The law declared by the Supreme Court shall be binding on all courts within the territory of India.

  • Article 142 - Enforcement of Decrees and Orders of Supreme Court and Orders as to Discovery, Etc.

    The Supreme Court is empowered to pass such decrees or make such orders as may be necessary for doing complete justice in any matter before it.

  • Article 143 - Power of President to Consult Supreme Court

    Authorizes the President to seek the opinion of the Supreme Court on questions of law or fact of public importance, which the Supreme Court may choose to answer.

  • Article 144 - Civil and Judicial Authorities to Act in Aid of the Supreme Court

    All authorities, civil and judicial, in the territory of India are required to act in aid of the Supreme Court.

  • Article 145 - Rules of Court, Etc.

    Empowers the Supreme Court to frame rules for regulating the practice and procedure of the Court, subject to laws made by Parliament.

  • Article 146 - Officers and Servants and the Expenses of the Supreme Court

    Provides for the appointment of officers and servants of the Supreme Court and for meeting the expenses of the Court from the Consolidated Fund of India.

  • Article 147 - Interpretation

    Clarifies that the term "this Constitution" refers to the Constitution as it stands, including any amendments made to it.
